2. Wave-particle duality

2.1 Wave or Particle

  • A pet, whether it’s a dog or a cat, loves to run around, but is only in one place at a time, it’s unique in the world coordinate system. Once a bullet goes out from a gun, it only goes in one place. That is how particle behave.
  • Jay call out and everyone heard the notice of morning meeting. The whole SEDAN membes go for a row, ripples on the lake. That how wave behave.
  • If the two modes of behaviour were swapped, a bullet is equivalent to an atomic bomb, only one get the meeting notice.

Well, in the macro world, everything behaves either as a wave or as a particle. There is no confusion.

2.2 Wave-particle

In quantum mechanics, wave-particle duality refers to the wave and particle property displayed by microscopic particles, which is one of the basic properties of microscopic particles. Microscopic particles sometimes exhibit wave behavior (when they are less significant) and sometimes particle behavior (when they are less significant), acting as wave or particle properties under different conditions. The wavelength and frequency of the wave means that it is extended in space and time. A particle can always be observed with a definite position and momentum in space and time. The complementarity principle states that quantum phenomena can be observed by one method or another conjugate method, but not by two mutually conjugate methods at the same time.
