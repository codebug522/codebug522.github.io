Dirac notations & density matrices

The direct notations and density matrices are used to describe the quantum states. Let $a,b \in \mathbb{C}^20 $ (2-dimensioal vector with complex numbers). And then we define:

ket: $|a\rangle=\binom{a_0}{a_1}$

bra: ${{\langle{b}|}={|b\rangle}^+={\binom{b_0}{b_1}}^+={{(b_0^*} {b_1^*)}}} $

bra-ket: $\langle{b}|{a}\rangle=a _0b^*+a_1b_1^*$=${\langle{b}|{a}|\rangle}^* \in \mathbb{C}^2$

ket-bra: $|a\rangle\langle{b}|=\begin{pmatrix}a_0b_0^* &a_0b_1^* \\ a_1b_0^* & a_1b_1^*\end{pmatrix}$

From the description of the quantum states, we define the pure state $|0\rangle=\binom{1}{0}$ and $|1\rangle=\binom{0}{1}$ which are orthogonal. That means we product $\langle{0}|{1}\rangle=0$.

and we prduct $|0\rangle|\langle{0}| = (1  0)\cdot\binom{1}{0}=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&0\end{pmatrix}$ and $|1\rangle|\langle{1}| = (0  1)\cdot\binom{0}{1}=\begin{pmatrix}0&0\\0&1\end{pmatrix}$

In general any matrix $\rho=\begin{pmatrix}\rho_{00} & \rho_{01} \\ \rho_{10} & \rho_{11} \end{pmatrix}=\rho_{00}\cdot|0\rangle|\langle{0}|+\rho_{01}\cdot|0\rangle|\langle{1}|+\rho_{10}\cdot|1\rangle|\langle{0}|+\rho_{11}\cdot|1\rangle|\langle{1}|$

All quantum states in any superpostion can be describe by density matrces, i.e. normalized, positive hermitian operators $\rho$

  • Normalization: $t_r(\rho)=1$
  • Positive: $\rho\geq{0}$, $\langle\psi|\rho|\psi\rangle\geq0$ ($|\psi\rangle$ all eigen values $\geq{0}$)
  • Hermitian: $\rho=\rho^+$

All quantum states are normalized $\langle\phi|\phi\rangle=1$

  • e.g. $|\phi\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|1\rangle+|0\rangle)$
